Introduction and Scope

This privacy policy describes how personal information is collected, used, and maintained when individuals interact with this website or request services. The practices described comply with applicable United States privacy regulations including the Federal Trade Commission Act Section 5, the Privacy Act of 1974, the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, and state-level privacy statutes such as the California Consumer Privacy Act as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act, the Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act, the Colorado Privacy Act, the Connecticut Data Privacy Act, and the Utah Consumer Privacy Act.

This website functions as an information resource about residential water systems and provides contact information for service inquiries. Personal information collection is limited to what is voluntarily provided through contact forms or direct communication channels.

Categories of Personal Information Collected

The following categories of personal information may be collected:

  • Contact identifiers: Name, email address, phone number, and mailing address when voluntarily provided through inquiry forms or direct communication
  • Service request information: Details about water system concerns, property location, and scheduling preferences when provided in service requests
  • Communication records: Content of messages, inquiries, and responses exchanged through email, phone, or contact forms
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, device information, and website interaction patterns collected automatically through standard web server logs

Sources of Personal Information

Personal information is obtained directly from individuals when they:

  • Complete and submit contact forms on this website
  • Send email inquiries to listed contact addresses
  • Make phone calls to listed contact numbers
  • Engage in correspondence about service requests or scheduling
  • Visit pages on this website, resulting in automatic collection of technical data through web server logs

No personal information is purchased from data brokers or obtained from third-party sources.

Purposes for Processing Personal Information

Personal information is used for the following purposes:

  • Service provision: Responding to inquiries, scheduling service visits, communicating about water system concerns, and providing requested information
  • Communication management: Maintaining records of correspondence, following up on service requests, and providing updates when requested
  • Administrative operations: Managing scheduling, maintaining contact records, and organizing service documentation
  • Website functionality: Analyzing how the website is accessed to maintain proper function and identify technical concerns
  • Legal compliance: Maintaining records as required by applicable business regulations and responding to lawful requests for information

Personal information is not used for targeted advertising, behavioral profiling, or sale to third parties.

Information Retention

Personal information is retained for the following periods:

  • Contact form submissions and email inquiries are retained for two years from the date of submission to maintain communication records
  • Service request records are kept for three years to maintain documentation of work performed and for reference in case of follow-up needs
  • Website access logs containing technical data are retained for 90 days for website maintenance purposes
  • Email correspondence is maintained according to standard business communication practices

When retention periods expire, information is deleted from active systems. Backup systems may retain information for additional time periods according to data backup schedules.

Information Sharing and Disclosure

Personal information is not sold, rented, or shared with third parties for their own purposes. Limited sharing occurs only in these specific circumstances:

  • Service providers: Email hosting services, website hosting providers, and scheduling systems that process information only as directed and under contractual confidentiality obligations
  • Legal requirements: When disclosure is required by law, court order, subpoena, or governmental request
  • Business continuity: In the event of business transfer, merger, or acquisition, where personal information may be included in transferred assets

No personal information is shared for marketing purposes, sold to data brokers, or used by third parties for their own business purposes.

Individual Rights Regarding Personal Information

Individuals have the following rights regarding their personal information:

  • Access: Request to know what personal information is maintained and obtain a copy of that information
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate personal information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of personal information, subject to legal retention requirements
  • Opt-out: Decline to receive non-essential communications
  • Data portability: Request personal information in a structured, machine-readable format where technically feasible

To exercise these rights, contact us using the information provided at the beginning of this policy. Requests will be responded to within 45 days. Verification of identity may be required before responding to requests.

No fees are charged for making requests, though excessive or repetitive requests may incur reasonable administrative costs. Individuals may designate an authorized agent to make requests on their behalf by providing written authorization.

Notice at Collection for California Residents

California residents are provided the following information at the time personal information is collected:

  • Categories of personal information collected are listed in the "Categories of Personal Information Collected" section above
  • Purposes for collection are detailed in the "Purposes for Processing Personal Information" section above
  • Personal information is not sold or shared for cross-context behavioral advertising
  • Personal information is not used for targeted advertising purposes
  • Sensitive personal information, if any is collected, is used only for permitted business purposes and not for profiling or inferring characteristics
  • Retention periods are specified in the "Information Retention" section above

California residents may exercise rights including the right to know, right to delete, right to correct, right to opt-out of sales (though no sales occur), and right to limit use of sensitive personal information. These rights are exercised using the contact information provided.

Information Security Measures

Technical and organizational measures are implemented to protect personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Secure website connections using encryption protocols
  • Access controls limiting who can view personal information
  • Regular security assessments of systems handling personal information
  • Secure deletion procedures when information retention periods expire
  • Contractual requirements for service providers handling personal information

While reasonable security measures are implemented, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure. The inherent risks of internet communication should be considered when providing personal information online.

Children's Information

This website is directed to adults seeking information about residential water systems. Personal information from children under 13 years of age is not knowingly collected in compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act. The website does not include features specifically directed to children, does not collect information known to be from children, and does not use persistent identifiers to track children.

If it becomes known that personal information from a child under 13 has been collected without parental consent, that information will be deleted from systems. Parents who believe their child has provided personal information may contact us using the information provided.

Automated Decision-Making

No automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects is conducted using personal information. Service scheduling, inquiry responses, and communication decisions involve human review and judgment.

Do Not Track and Global Privacy Control

This website respects Do Not Track signals and Global Privacy Control signals from browsers. When these signals are detected, optional analytics and non-essential tracking are disabled for that visit. The website continues to function normally when these signals are present.

Changes to Privacy Practices

Privacy practices may be modified over time to reflect changes in operations, legal requirements, or technology. When changes occur, the "Last Updated" date at the beginning of this policy is revised. Significant changes that materially alter how personal information is handled will be communicated through prominent notice on the website.

Continued use of the website or provision of personal information after changes indicates acceptance of modified privacy practices.

Additional Information

This privacy policy applies specifically to information collected through this website and direct communications. It does not cover information collected through other means or by other entities.

For more information, please use the contact details above.
